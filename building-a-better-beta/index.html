Building a Better Beta

June 17, 2011Charlie
  • beta
  • learning

Monotask is a curious bit of software. Most software extends your computer in some novel way. Monotask, though? Essentially, Monotask takes what your computer’s supposed to do (go online without any issues), and it breaks it. Then, after a bit, it fixes it again.

Because Monotask is breaking your computer, we’ve been very careful about getting it out to beta testers. Actually, no. That’s a nice-sounding lie. I’ll speak for myself here: I’ve been scared to get it out to beta testers. And that’s the first step of a dangerous spiral:

  1. I’m anxious about getting it out to users and having it break their computer.
  2. I don’t send it out to beta users.
  3. We don’t get feedback on the software.
  4. Having no feedback from outside users, we’re limited in how we can fix it.
  5. GO TO STEP 1.

That’s a cycle we need to break.

So. Beginning as soon as I publish this post, I’m going to reach out to the next five beta users on our beta list. The goal: Get them using Monotask, on a free account, and giving us feedback on how the client app works.

I’ll try to post my exact e-mail message in my next post, but here’s the crux of what I need to convey:

  • Monotask works on our own computers
  • Monotask should work on your computer
  • here’s the download link
  • we’re trying to find the rough edges where Monotask isn’t working
  • if it doesn’t work and you need to uninstall it, here are some instructions

Whether we get good feedback or not, my intention is to send it to five more beta testers a day, until we’ve exhausted the list or launched out of beta.

If you’d like to be a beta tester (or if you’re a beta tester and haven’t heard from me yet when you read this), send me an e-mail and let me know: charlie@monotask.com. I’d love to hear from you.
