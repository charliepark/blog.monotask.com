One of the things that I most admire about Eric Ries and the whole “Lean Startup” movement is how much of a focus there is on learning. People often interpret “lean” to mean “cheap”, or misattribute the quote “fail fast”. As Eric says, though, it’s not about failing faster, but about learning faster.

This post isn’t a claim that we’ve figured out how to learn faster, but that it’s something we aspire to do, and it’s something I’m hoping to write more about in the near future. We’ll look at things like:

  • how do we learn?
  • what kinds of questions should we ask?
  • what research methods should we employ?
  • what do we then do with the data?
  • how do we learn how to learn? (how do we improve the process?)

I’m looking forward to exploring all this more, and to sharing it with you. For now, though … back to work.

Hello, world.

We just wanted to give a quick intro, and then get back to work building Monotask.

Who We Are

We’re Charlie (@charliepark) and Tony (@mogrify).

We’re building Monotask. It’s attention management software. That means it helps you get to work. We’ll write more about it in future posts.

What We’re Doing Here

We’ve been working on Monotask for a while now. A few years, honestly. We’re getting ready to share it with the world, but we’ve been hitting a few roadblocks. This blog is a place where we can update beta testers about the app and what we’re working on.

It’s also a place where we can note things that we need to address with the app, log feedback from users, and, in general, be more transparent.

Starting a startup is hard, especially, when it’s something you can’t focus on full-time. As we go through it, we want to share some of the lessons we’re learning, the challenges we’re facing, and the victories we’re celebrating.

That’s It For Now

We’ll be posting again, soon. We just wanted to get this first post out there, to say hi and all that. If you want to check out Monotask, you can do that at Monotask.com.

More soon.
